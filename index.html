The Philosophy

A concept 12 years in the making

Every discipline that earns a seat at the boardroom table has a word for its central quality. Finance has liquidity. Law has precedent. Strategy has moat. HR has never had its word. Until now.

Humacity
humanus (Latin: human) + -acity (as in tenacity, audacity, veracity)

The fundamental quality of human capital in an organisation. Every organisation has it, whether they measure it or not. It determines how an organisation performs, who it attracts, what it is capable of, and what it becomes. Most organisations have never named it. None of them have quantified it.

"The Humacity of this team is declining. We need to improve our Humacity position."

About

The practitioner behind the work

The first time I felt it, I was frustrated. HR walked into a leadership meeting with real work, real insight, real data. The room moved on in seven minutes. That frustration stayed with me for years.

Then I started feeling something worse: embarrassed. Not for myself, but for the function. For the HRBP who had prepared for three days and got three minutes. For the HR Head who knew exactly what was breaking in the organisation and could not get anyone to listen. The function carried the emotional weight of the entire org and had no language to make that weight visible.

That is when the frustration and embarrassment became something clearer: the problem was never the people. It was the language. HR was speaking in engagement scores and retention percentages. The boardroom spoke in currency. Nobody had built the translation layer.

So I built it. Humacity is the philosophical framework. Revenue Fluent is the first product that makes it usable by any HR practitioner, not just the ones with data science teams. The Situation Room is next: a space where HR practitioners can think through real-time decisions using frameworks, not just instincts.

HR people carry the emotional weight of every organisation. Nobody has ever quantified it. That is what I am trying to change.
